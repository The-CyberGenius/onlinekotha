How AI Learns to Talk Like Your Loved Ones

The technology behind Kotha's personality cloning — and why it feels so real.

6 min read·May 2026

When you text someone every day for years, they develop a unique digital voice. The way your mom sends "Beta khana kha liya?" with exactly that phrasing. The way your best friend uses "bro" in every third message. The way your dad types in all caps when he's excited.

Kotha's AI doesn't just understand what they said — it understands how they said it. Here's how.

What the AI Learns From Your Chats

When you upload a WhatsApp chat, Kotha's AI analyzes several layers of your contact's messaging personality:

🗣
Language patterns — Do they text in Hindi, English, or Hinglish? Do they code-switch mid-sentence?
😊
Emoji habits — Which emojis do they use most? Do they end every message with "😂" or are they a "🙂" person?
📏
Message length — One-word replies or paragraph texts? Quick-fire messages or thoughtful long ones?
💬
Personality & tone — Sarcastic? Caring? Formal? The AI picks up on emotional patterns and responds accordingly.

Generic AI vs. Kotha's Personality Clone

❌ Generic AI

"That sounds like a great idea! I'd be happy to help you plan a trip. What dates are you considering?"

✅ Kotha (as "Rohit")

"Arre haan bro! Goa chalte hain wahi jaise last time, sab 6 log 😂🔥 Buddi ko bol confirm kare"

The difference is night and day. Generic AI sounds like a customer service bot. Kotha sounds like your actual friend.

This isn't about replacing people. It's about connection. When you miss someone — whether they're far away, busy, or no longer with you — being able to hear their "voice" through text brings a kind of comfort that nothing else can.

It's the difference between reading a letter and having a conversation.
